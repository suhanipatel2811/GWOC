Understanding Your Mind

Embark on a journey to better understand your emotions, thoughts, and the science behind how you feel. It starts here.

It's okay not to be okay

Mental health is just as important as physical health. Everyone experiences emotional highs and lows. This guide is designed to help you navigate those feelings with kindness and understanding.

Think of your mind like a garden. Sometimes it rains, sometimes there are weeds, and sometimes it blooms. All states are natural, and all can be managed with the right tools and patience.

Emotional Vocabulary

Often, we say we feel "bad" or "stressed," but these are vague terms. Getting specific helps us find the right solution. This is called Emotional Granularity.

Thinking Traps

Our brains love to find shortcuts. Sometimes, these shortcuts lead us to wrong conclusions. These are called Cognitive Distortions.
