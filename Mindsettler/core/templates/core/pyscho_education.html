2 The Biological Basics

Your body has an built-in alarm system designed to keep you safe. Sometimes, it gets a little too sensitive. Here are the three main states of your nervous system response:

sports_mma

Fight

Your body prepares to confront danger. Muscles tense, heart races, and you might feel irritable or angry.

sprint

Flight

The urge to run away or avoid. You might feel restless, anxious, or have racing thoughts about escaping.

ac_unit

Freeze

When danger feels inescapable, you shut down. You might feel numb, disconnected, or unable to move.

3 Emotional Vocabulary

Often, we say we feel "bad" or "stressed," but these are vague terms. Getting specific helps us find the right solution. This is called Emotional Granularity.

Instead of "Stressed", try:

4 Thinking Traps

Our brains love to take shortcuts. Sometimes, these shortcuts lead us to wrong conclusions. These are called Cognitive Distortions.

thunderstorm

Catastrophizing

Assuming the worst possible outcome will happen.

The Trap: "I made a mistake at work. I'm going to get fired and lose my house."
The Reality: "I made a mistake. It's uncomfortable, but I can fix it and learn."
contrast

Black & White Thinking

Viewing situations as only good or bad, with no middle ground.

The Trap: "If I don't get an A on this test, I'm a total failure."
The Reality: "I can do well in some areas and struggle in others. That's normal."
